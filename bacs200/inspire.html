Picture this — a seemingly endless sheet of ice, a loud scratch of the ice, then magic. Jason Brown, a now Olympic figure skater, was right in front of me doing a triple axle jump at the ice rink my sister had been training at.

I had learned to skate at a young age of 5 but stopped a few years later. However, once seeing Jason take off with such ease, grace, athleticism, it inspired me to skate again. I soon watched him then in events all around the world, from France to Japan and everywhere in between. And now I’m skating 10 years later while at the University of Northern Colorado.

He is an extremely friendly and humble person. You’d never know he was an Olympic skater just talking with him. He’s your typical 20 something year old, who had been going to college in Colorado Springs at UCCS, the University of Colorado Springs, Colorado.

What makes him unique is his dedication to keep going even though times can be tough. Jason is one of the few Olympic skaters who hasn’t been able to do a quad jump. But, due to his extreme artistic ability he’s still been able to score near the top of the pack. He’s still trying to land the quad and everyone keeps telling him he’ll never make it. But, I have seen him land it. It’s just a matter of time before he adds it to his arsenal. With his artistic ability, determination, athleticism, and then added to the quad, he would be unstoppable.

Jason is someone whom I look up to, and someone who inspires me. I can only hope that one day I can achieve greatness, or even a portion of what Jason’s achieved. (I’m on the left at a young age of ~10 :) )
